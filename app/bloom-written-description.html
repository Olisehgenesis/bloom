Bloom — Written System Description

Real-time money streaming on Celo, powered by Superfluid and GoodDollar.

Applicant: Mwijusya Oliseh  ·  System: Bloom  ·  Ref: MoICT&NG/DIEG/2025-26/001-AF  ·  Submission: May 2026

1. Executive Summary

Bloom turns one-off payments into continuous, per-second money streams. A sender — an employer, parent, diaspora relative, NGO, or Government programme — funds an account once and value flows to the recipient every second of every day for a chosen duration. Recipients off-ramp to MTN or Airtel Mobile Money whenever they need cash. Bloom is built on the Celo blockchain (a mobile-first, low-fee, carbon-negative Layer 1 designed for Africa), uses Superfluid for the streaming primitive, GoodDollar (G$) as a stable unit of account, Uniswap V3 for token routing, and Fonbnk for mobile-money on/off-ramps. The application is a Progressive Web App (PWA) — installable on low-end Android phones, works offline for viewing, and signs in with a wallet (no bank account required).

2. Problem & Motivation

Most payments in Uganda — salaries, allowances, school fees, family upkeep, pensions, social cash transfers (SAGE, NUSAF), and gig/freelance income — arrive as large, infrequent lump sums. Workers wait a full month for pay, parents send school fees in one painful transfer, diaspora relatives wire money once and hope it lasts, and beneficiaries of Government or NGO programmes queue for batched payouts that lose value to agent fees. Between payments, people run out of cash for daily needs and fall back on predatory short-term lenders. Mobile money solved access to digital value but not cadence — funds still move as discrete, all-or-nothing transfers. Bloom closes that gap.

3. Solution Overview

What Bloom does

  • Accepts deposits in any supported token, or directly from MTN/Airtel Mobile Money via Fonbnk.
  • Auto-swaps the deposit to G$ through Uniswap V3 (single-hop or CELO two-hop).
  • Opens a Superfluid stream of G$ to one or many recipients for a chosen duration.
  • Lets recipients watch their balance increase per second and off-ramp to mobile money on demand.
  • Records every deposit, swap, stream and claim on the public Celo ledger — auditable end-to-end.

Why blockchain

  • Per-second value transfer is only practical on a programmable ledger.
  • Immutable audit trail for every shilling — no bespoke reporting tools.
  • Sybil-resistant identity via GoodDollar face verification eliminates ghost recipients.
  • Self-custody: senders and recipients hold their own keys; Bloom never touches funds.
  • Low fees: Celo transactions cost a fraction of a US cent.

4.3 Deposit and stream flow

  1. Quote — the PWA calls Uniswap V3 Factory off-chain to find a pool (tokenIn → G$ direct, or tokenIn → CELO → G$ two-hop) and reads slot0() to estimate minGDOut.
  2. Approve — the user signs an ERC-20 approve() letting BloomProxy spend their tokenIn.
  3. Deposit — a single call to bloom.deposit(token, amount, splitBps, minGDOut) pulls the token, swaps to G$ via SwapRouter02, and credits the user's Bloom balance.
  4. Stream — the user calls bloom.startStream(recipient, duration), which opens a Superfluid flow at flowRate = balance ÷ duration seconds.
  5. Claim / off-ramp — the recipient's G$ balance accrues per second and can be redeemed to UGX via Fonbnk at any time.
Mobile-money top-up: the PWA requests a signed Fonbnk widget URL from a Next.js API route that mints a short-lived JWT containing the user's wallet address. Fonbnk delivers the purchased token directly to that wallet — no server-side correlation, no custody risk.

7. Interoperability

  • RESTful API — Next.js API routes expose JSON endpoints (e.g. /api/auth/siwe, /api/fonbnk/widget-url, /api/wallet).
  • Mobile money — live with MTN MoMo and Airtel Money via Fonbnk; covers all major Uganda telcos.
  • NIRA / National ID — NIN can be bound to a wallet via a one-time attestation endpoint (KYC tier upgrade), without storing the NIN itself.
  • URA — disbursement history is exportable as CSV or via REST for tax-compliant reporting.
  • NITA-U — the stateless API tier can be redeployed behind NITA-U gateway / private cloud for inter-agency integration.
  • Standards — EIP-4361 (SIWE), ERC-20, ERC-777/4626-style flows via Superfluid, OAuth2/JWT for partner integrations.

8. Use Cases

8.1 Citizen & private use

  • Salary smoothing — employers stream monthly pay continuously instead of one lump on payday.
  • Family upkeep / diaspora remittances — senders abroad stream school fees and groceries directly to relatives' wallets, who off-ramp to MoMo as needed.
  • Freelancer & gig payments — clients pay developers, designers and drivers per second over a project's duration; disputes drop because work and pay are in sync.
  • Rent & subscriptions — tenants stream rent daily; landlords get predictable cash-flow and instant arrears visibility.

8.2 Government & institutional use

  • Social cash transfers (SAGE, NUSAF, emergency relief) — Government funds a Bloom stream once, beneficiaries receive value continuously, every transfer is on-chain auditable, and Sybil-resistant identity prevents duplicates and ghost beneficiaries.
  • Civil-servant allowances — per-diems, field allowances and per-hour payments streamed in real time, reducing reconciliation overhead.
  • Pensions — continuous, transparent pension disbursement with on-chain proof-of-life via periodic GoodDollar re-verification.
  • NGO & donor programmes — donors fund a programme once; recipients receive verifiable, time-bounded streams; donors can audit utilisation in real time.
